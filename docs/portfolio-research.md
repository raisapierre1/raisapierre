## 2. Portfolio Platforms — Comparison

### Framer (Recommended for Designers)
- Designer-friendly, no-code, modern interactive templates
- Easy implementation of animations and micro-interactions
- Real-time collaboration support
- Growing rapidly (~171,000+ sites)
- **Limitation:** Less robust CMS, fewer SEO tools than Webflow

### Webflow
- Enterprise-grade CMS, best-in-class SEO tools
- Advanced localization, powerful animations
- Largest user base (~590,000+ sites)
- **Limitation:** Steep learning curve, templates can feel dated, overkill for simple portfolios

### Custom-Built (Astro / Next.js)
- Full creative control and brand differentiation
- Astro ships zero JS by default = perfect performance scores
- Can leverage cutting-edge tech (Three.js, Framer Motion, WebGL)
- **Limitation:** Requires development skills or AI assistance, longer time-to-launch

### Other Options
- **Squarespace** — Good design templates, moderate SEO, user-friendly
- **WordPress** — Highly flexible with plugins, steeper learning curve

### Recommendation for Raisa
Given your 16+ years of design expertise and AI-assisted development: **Astro + Tailwind CSS** gives you the best blend of performance, SEO, and creative freedom. Alternatively, **Framer** is the fastest path to a polished, modern portfolio if you want to focus purely on design.

## 3. What Makes a Senior Portfolio Stand Out

### Junior vs. Senior Portfolio — Key Differences

A junior portfolio shows *what* was designed. A senior portfolio shows *why* decisions were made, *how* they impacted the business, and *what* was learned.

**Senior/Lead portfolios emphasize:**
- Strategic thinking and design direction
- Business impact with metrics (conversion rates, revenue, time saved)
- Team leadership and mentorship evidence
- Cross-functional collaboration with engineering, product, stakeholders
- Design systems and scalability
- Research strategy integration

### Essential Content

**Homepage:** Clean, professional. Signals business impact immediately. Hiring managers spend 3-10 seconds scanning before deciding to go deeper.

**Case Studies (3-5 detailed):** Each should follow this structure:
1. Problem Statement — Clearly framed business or user problem
2. Research & Analysis — User insights, competitive analysis, data
3. Design Process — Early explorations, iterations, key challenges
4. Key Decisions — Why specific choices were made, constraints navigated
5. Results & Impact — Metrics: engagement %, revenue, time saved, NPS
6. Reflection — What was learned, what would change

**About Page:** Your story, career arc, design philosophy, what you bring to teams.

**For Lead/Manager level, also include:**
- Design system governance and roadmap examples
- How you scaled research across teams
- Executive influence and board communication
- Mentorship approach and team growth stories

### Portfolio Design Standards
- Must reflect your design excellence — everything polished
- Fast loading (< 2s), mobile-responsive, accessible (WCAG AA)
- Clear information hierarchy and easy navigation
- Professional imagery and typography
